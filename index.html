the cabazon dinossaurs

ON FEBRUARY 13, 1870, Southern Pacific Railroad DISCOVERED A TAR PIT THAT THEY NAMED EL Cabazone TAR PIT.

ON NOVEMBER 15, 1958, WITHIN EL CABAZONE TAR PIT, Knott's Berry Farm FOUND SOMETHING EXTRAORDINARY— DINOSAUR fossils IN THE SAME AREA FROM THE THREE DIFFERENT PERIODS OF THE MESOZOIC ERA.

THEY FOUND A Tyrannosaurus tetcinwis FROM THE LATE CRETACEOUS PERIOD (68-66 MA), A Brontosaurus nehululu FROM THE LATE JURASSIC PERIOD (156.3-146.8 MA), AND FINALLY, THE OLDEST FOSSIL EVER FOUND IN THE WORLD, A Nyasasaurus iikqa FROM THE MIDDLE TRIASSIC PERIOD (247.2-237 MA).

JACINTAURUS IIKQA'S DISCOVERY IS NOT JUST A BIG ACHIEVEMENT FOR OUR NATIONAL scientific AND archeological HERITAGE BUT A BIG STEP FOR THE DEVELOPMENT OF A COMPLETE UNDERSTANDING OF OUR scientific AND universal history AND OF THE origin of life.

TODAY, HERE YOU WILL FIND EXACT REPLICAS BUILT BY Claude K. Bell OF THE BRONTOSAURUS NEHULULU KNOWN AS DINNY (1964), AND OF THE TYRANNOSAURUS TETCIÑWIS KNOWN AS MR. REX (1981). THE JACINTAURUS IIKQA IS STILL IN THE PROCESS OF SCIENTIFIC RECONSTRUCTION, SCHEDULED TO BE ON DISPLAY IN 2012.
